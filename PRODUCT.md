# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Factory workers operating a horizontal touchscreen kiosk on a nut and washer counting machine. They work quickly in a very bright, dusty factory environment, often wearing gloves, often viewing the screen from farther away than a desk UI, and frequently glancing rather than reading carefully. The display is highly reflective.

## Product Purpose

Nut Counter helps operators count trays of nuts or washers quickly and confidently. A normal cycle is: place tray, choose Nut or Washer when needed, wait for the count, remove tray. Some runs repeat the same part type for many trays; some runs switch part type often. Success means the operator can see the count immediately, recover from errors quickly, and tap the right control without slowing the line.

## Brand Personality

Strong, clear, fast, high-contrast. The interface should feel like reliable industrial equipment: practical, direct, and calm under pressure.

## Anti-references

Do not make it look like a SaaS dashboard, a mobile app, a decorative cyber interface, or a colorful consumer UI. Avoid too many buttons, tiny touch targets, unnecessary data panels, ornamental motion, and loud saturated color. Text does not need to be huge everywhere; only the count and critical state messages should dominate.

## Design Principles

1. Count first: the result or required next action must be the most visible thing on the screen.
2. Glove-safe operation: primary touch targets must be large, separated, and forgiving.
3. Industrial clarity: high contrast, low glare, plain labels, minimal visual noise.
4. Fixed camera truth: the video area stays 1:1 and remains the visual anchor of the machine.
5. Error immediacy: faults must interrupt ambiguity and be understood at a glance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design for bright reflective screens, dusty conditions, gloved touch, and glance-based reading from distance. Use high contrast, large critical typography, large touch targets, non-color-only state communication, minimal motion, and clear Thai labels. Assume operators may be moving quickly and may not read secondary text carefully.
